Text Description

  • Sun

    The Solar System is the Sun and all the objects that orbit around it. The Sun is orbited by planets, asteroids, comets and other things. It is Billions of years old. The Sun is a star.

  • Mercury

    Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System. It is the closest planet to the sun. It makes one trip around the Sun once every 87.969 days.

  • Venus

    Venus is the second planet from the sun.Venus is sometimes called the sister planet of Earth as they are quite similar in size and gravity.Venus has no moons. Venus spins very slowly on its axis and it spins in the opposite direction to the other planets.

  • Earth

    Earth is the planet we live on. It is the third planet from the sun. It is the only planet known to have life on it.The large mass of the sun makes Earth move around it, just as the mass of Earth makes the moon move around it.

  • Mars

    Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun in the Solar System and the second-smallest solid planet. Mars is rocky and cold, with polar ice caps of frozen water. It has the largest volcano in the Solar System.

  • jupiter

    Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System. It is the fifth planet from the Sun. Jupiter is a gas giant, both because it is so large and made up of gas.

  • Saturn

    Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun in the Solar System. It is the second largest planet in the Solar System, after Jupiter.Saturn is best known for its planetary rings which are easy to see with a telescope.Scientists believe that the rings are material left after a moon broke apart.

  • Uranus

    Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun in the Solar System. It is a gas giant. It is the third largest planet in the solar system. The planet is made of ice, gases and liquid metal. Its atmosphere contains hydrogen, helium and methane.

  • Neptune

    Neptune, is the eighth and last planet from the Sun in the Solar System. It is a gas giant. It is the fourth largest planet and third heaviest. Neptune has four rings which are hard to see from the Earth.
